Every drink you take triggers a chain reaction inside your body. By the time you wake up, the damage is already done — unless you stopped it before it started.

01 — Vitamin Depletion

B-Vitamins Are Destroyed

Alcohol is a diuretic — it forces your kidneys to flush out B1, B2, B6, and B12 at an accelerated rate. These vitamins power your brain, nervous system, and energy production. When they're gone, you feel it everywhere.

02 — Oxidative Stress

Your Liver Is Working Overtime

As your liver metabolizes alcohol, it produces acetaldehyde — a toxic compound that causes inflammation, nausea, and headaches. Antioxidants neutralize it, but alcohol depletes those too. The result: the toxic buildup wins.

03 — Electrolyte Loss

Your Body Can't Recover Without These

Potassium, magnesium, and zinc are flushed out alongside the B-vitamins. Without them, your muscles cramp, your head pounds, and your brain runs in fog. This is what a hangover actually is.
