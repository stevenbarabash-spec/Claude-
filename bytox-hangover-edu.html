Fact #1

Alcohol doesn't just dehydrate you — it depletes 11 key vitamins & minerals from your body.

Every drink flushes out B1, B6, B12, Zinc, Magnesium, and more — the same nutrients your body uses for energy production, nerve function, and inflammation control. That's why you wake up exhausted, foggy, and aching.

Fact #2

By the time you feel thirsty, you're already significantly dehydrated.

Alcohol is a diuretic — it tells your kidneys to push out fluid faster than you're taking it in. You lose up to 4x more fluid than normal. The headache, dry mouth, and dizziness? All dehydration signals.

Fact #3

Your liver produces a toxic byproduct called Acetaldehyde — and it's 30x more toxic than alcohol itself.

When your body breaks down alcohol, it creates acetaldehyde before converting it to harmless acetic acid. That buildup causes nausea, flushing, sweating, and vomiting. It's essentially a toxic attack on your cells.

Drinking water AFTER won't save you. Recovery starts BEFORE and DURING drinking.
