GLP-1 stands for Glucagon-Like Peptide-1. It's a hormone your body naturally produces — and it's one of the most powerful signals your brain receives when deciding whether you're hungry, full, or somewhere in between. Most people have never heard of it. Most people are also running low on it.

The Science

Here's what GLP-1
actually does.

When you eat, your gut releases GLP-1 into your bloodstream. It travels to your brain and pancreas and triggers three critical responses.

01 — Signals Fullness

Tells Your Brain You're Full

GLP-1 travels directly to the hypothalamus — the hunger control center — and switches off the "keep eating" signal. When GLP-1 is low, that signal never turns off.

02 — Slows Digestion

Keeps You Satisfied Longer

GLP-1 slows gastric emptying — food moves through your stomach more slowly, so you feel full longer and blood sugar stays stable instead of spiking and crashing.

03 — Regulates Insulin

Stabilizes Blood Sugar

GLP-1 triggers insulin release only when blood sugar is elevated — a precise, safe response that prevents the spikes and crashes that drive cravings.
