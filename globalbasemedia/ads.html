Google Ads FAQs

Questions, answered straight

If we somehow didn't answer your question here, we definitely want to hear it when you get your free marketing plan.

What does a Google Ads agency do?
A Google Ads agency manages every part of your paid search — strategy, keyword research, ad copy, campaign setup, bid optimization, landing pages, conversion tracking, and reporting. The best ones optimize for real business outcomes like booked jobs and revenue, not just clicks, by connecting Google Ads to your CRM. At Global Base Media, we manage Search, Performance Max, Display, YouTube, and Local Services Ads for local service businesses — and wire every lead into your CRM so we optimize on booked jobs, not vanity metrics.
How much does it cost to hire a Google Ads agency?
Cost depends on your ad spend and how much you want us to manage — campaign management, landing pages, tracking, and follow-up. Instead of a one-size-fits-all price, we scope a plan to your goals and budget on a quick consultation. What's always true: no long-term lock-in, you own your account, and every dollar is tracked so you see exactly what it returns. Call (561) 556-9190 or book a consultation for a quote.
What's the difference between Google Ads and Google AdWords?
They're the same platform. Google renamed "AdWords" to "Google Ads" in July 2018 to reflect its expansion beyond text search ads into Display, Shopping, YouTube, App, Discovery, and Performance Max. Any reference to "AdWords" is just the old name for what's now Google Ads.
How long does it take to see results from Google Ads?
Most businesses see their first leads within 1–2 weeks of launch, with optimal performance around 60–90 days as Google's machine learning gathers enough conversion data. The first 30 days focus on data collection, account structure, and baseline optimization; months 2–3 are when automated bidding gains signal and cost-per-lead improves. Timelines vary with account history, budget, conversion volume, and how quickly your customers book.
What makes a good Google Ads agency?
A good agency has current Google Partner certification, transparent real-time reporting with direct account access, and optimizes for business outcomes — booked jobs and revenue — over vanity metrics. Global Base Media is a Google Partner: you own your account and login, contracts are month-to-month, and we report in dollars. Red flags to avoid: long lock-in contracts, agencies that own your account, guaranteed results before seeing your account, or no direct account access.
Should I hire a Google Ads agency or manage ads in-house?
Hire an agency when you don't have dedicated PPC expertise, want to scale quickly, or need specialized skills — conversion optimization, tracking, multi-channel — that a single hire can't provide. In-house can make sense if you have an experienced PPC specialist, a healthy monthly budget, and time for daily optimization. For most service businesses, an agency is the better ROI: you skip the hiring process and your team stays focused on serving customers.
What businesses does Global Base Media specialize in?
We specialize in local service businesses — dental, HVAC, roofing, landscaping, salons, med spas, and home services. We understand how these customers search ("emergency AC repair," "dentist near me"), how to turn a click into a booked appointment, and how to optimize for booked jobs and revenue rather than raw lead volume.
How do I know if my campaigns are performing well?
Measure by business impact, not just platform metrics. The primary KPIs are cost per acquisition (CPA), return on ad spend (ROAS), and revenue attributed to Google Ads. Conversion rate and click-through rate help gauge campaign health, but the real question is whether campaigns produce booked jobs. Red flags: optimizing for cost-per-lead without tracking which leads actually book, campaigns running 90+ days with no structural changes, no CRM/offline conversion tracking, and high spend on broad keywords with few conversions.
What's the difference between Google Ads and SEO?
Both help you appear in Google, but differently. Google Ads delivers immediate paid visibility (above organic results, marked "Sponsored"), pay-per-click, with full control of messaging and targeting. SEO earns organic rankings over time with no per-click cost but less control. The strongest strategy uses both: Ads for immediate leads and to test which keywords convert, SEO for compounding lower-cost traffic. We offer both — most clients start with Ads for immediate calls, then add SEO within a few months.
Does Google Ads work for local service businesses?
Yes — it's one of the most effective channels because it captures high-intent demand: people actively searching for a service right now. Unlike social ads that interrupt, Google Search Ads and Local Services Ads appear exactly when someone needs you. The key is optimizing for booked jobs, not raw lead volume — which means CRM-connected tracking and value-based bidding so your budget chases revenue.
How does Google Ads bidding work?
Google runs an auction: advertisers bid on keywords and Google decides placement from a mix of bid amount and Quality Score (a 1–10 rating of expected click-through rate, ad relevance, and landing-page experience). Higher Quality Scores can mean lower costs and better positions, so the highest bidder doesn't always win. We layer automated bidding — Target CPA, Target ROAS, Maximize Conversions and Value — with manual guardrails and your CRM data so Google optimizes for revenue, not just conversion count.
Can I switch agencies without losing my data?
Yes. If you own your Google Ads account (you should — it's a red flag if your current agency owns it), switching doesn't affect your campaign data, conversion history, Quality Scores, or account standing. Your new agency simply gets manager access and all historical data carries over. We audit your existing account, find quick wins, and improve performance without disrupting live campaigns.

Most clients see their first optimizations live within two weeks and measurable improvement within 30 days.
